#61 Solid ash hardwood floor refinishing in a Del Mar bluff-top estate overlooking Torrey Pines State Beach near Carmel Valley Road. These new photos capture the transformation of a sun-faded ash floor in a coastal home just south of Del Mar Heights and Torrey Pines State Reserve. Using dust-containment sanding and careful repair work, the floors were restored to a natural clear finish that brightens the long hallways and living spaces. This project highlights ash hardwood’s unique grain while tying craftsmanship to one of San Diego’s most recognizable ocean-view neighborhoods.

#62 Solid hickory hardwood floor refinishing in Rancho Santa Fe’s Fairbanks Ranch, on the edge of Solana Beach and just north of Del Mar near Via de la Valle. This elaborate estate featured vintage hickory floors that required a complete sand and refinish. We prepared the surface with dust-containment equipment, tested stain samples on site, and worked with the owners to select a custom finish that brought out the rich tones of the hickory. The project was completed with a Bona Traffic HD commercial-grade polyurethane, leaving the floors durable, elegant, and perfectly matched to the style of this Rancho Santa Fe estate.

#63 Maple hardwood floor refinishing in Carmel Valley, San Diego. This Lauzon maple floor was fully sanded with dust-containment equipment to keep the home clean and safe. After preparation, Bona AmberSeal was applied to add a warm tone to the naturally pale maple, followed by a commercial-grade finish that brought the surface to perfection. This mid-county San Diego project shows how even well-worn maple floors can be restored to a rich, elegant look with the right process and products.

#64 Rancho Santa Fe wood floor refinishing. Solid hickory floors sanded dust-free and site finished with a custom stain. Butcher block countertops refinished to match, restoring all wood surfaces in this San Diego estate.

#65 La Jolla Shores oak floor refinishing with major termite damage repair. In this mid-century home near Scripps Pier at the bottom of Torrey Pines Road, an entire section of vintage oak flooring was replaced and blended with new boards. After repairs, the floor was sanded dust-free, stained nutmeg, and finished with a satin sheen for a seamless historical restoration.

#66 Santa Luz engineered oak floor refinishing on the east side of Carmel Valley, just north of Highway 56 near Black Mountain and Rancho Peñasquitos. This home’s sun-faded engineered oak was sanded dust-free, stained in a classic brown to highlight the grain, and finished with a satin sheen for lasting beauty. Another example of precise hardwood floor restoration in coastal North County San Diego.

#67 Douglas fir hardwood floor refinishing in San Diego’s Golden Hill historic district, near South Park, North Park, Bankers Hill, and the Gaslamp Quarter. This vintage softwood flooring, common in older San Diego apartments and historic homes, was worn and dull from decades of use. We fully restored the Douglas fir with dust-free sanding and a satin finish, bringing back the natural warmth and character of this rare softwood. Another Golden Hill apartment project showing expert hardwood restoration in San Diego’s historic neighborhoods.

#68 Solid red oak floor refinishing in Rancho Santa Fe Farms near Carmel Valley and Del Mar, just off the 56 freeway in North County San Diego. This 1980s red oak had turned orange from old oil-based polyurethane. The first photo shows the Bona dust-containment vacuum set up before sanding, while the second reveals the transformation—refinished with rotary equipment to a creamy, modern, beach-inspired tone with a light white haze. The result is a bright, contemporary look that perfectly suits this Rancho Santa Fe estate.

#69 Solid white oak floor restoration in a Del Mar ocean-view rental home. This 1980 installation had turned extremely orange from old polyurethane, with severe sun damage, water damage, and termite damage. Our team replaced damaged boards, sanded the floor clean, and provided custom stain samples. The homeowner chose a light water-based sealer from Bona to keep the oak natural and bright, finished with a commercial-grade satin sheen. The before photos show heavy damage, while the after reveals a flawless, flat, brand-new look—perfect for a coastal property overlooking the Pacific in Del Mar.

#70 Carbonized bamboo floor refinishing in University City, San Diego. This 30-year-old, horizontally laminated bamboo floor had severe sun fading and surface wear but remained structurally sound. Located just south of UTC, near Mount Soledad, La Jolla, and Pacific Beach, the home sits in a classic residential neighborhood off the 52 Freeway. We sanded away the tough aluminum oxide finish with full dust-containment equipment, then restored the bamboo with a traditional oil sealer to deepen its natural carbonized color. A commercial-grade finish completed the project, leaving the floors rich, durable, and stunning—proof that even decades-old bamboo can be brought back to life.

#71 Red oak hardwood floor refinishing in San Diego’s College Area, near SDSU, Lemon Grove, and Rolando off the 94 Freeway. This vintage apartment floor was blackened and dirty from decades of renters and heavy use. The property manager chose restoration over replacement, and the results are dramatic—a complete transformation from dark, mucky boards to bright, pristine oak. Another cost-effective San Diego hardwood floor refinishing project that shows the value of restoration done right.

#72 Mission Beach hardwood floor refinishing in San Diego. This vintage random-width white oak floor with walnut plugs had suffered from years of sun, salt, and coastal moisture. Located in South Mission Beach off Mission Boulevard near the jetty and Pacific Beach, the floor was fully restored with dust-free sanding and finished natural with a durable commercial-grade coating. The result is a flawless, beach-ready floor designed to withstand renters and heavy boardwalk traffic—an excellent example of expert coastal hardwood floor refinishing in San Diego’s Crown Point, Mission Bay, Bay Park, Bay Ho, and Point Loma neighborhoods.

#73 Brazilian cherry floor restoration – Coronado Island, First Avenue This Coronado Island home on First Avenue overlooks downtown San Diego with spectacular bay and city views. #74 Wide plank French oak refinishing – Olivenhain, Encinitas This Olivenhain home near Encinitas Ranch and horse country had character-grade, wide plank, long length French oak with a 6 mm top layer that was sun-faded and worn, likely from its original oiled finish. We sanded the floor completely flat, removed the wire brushing, and contained all dust throughout the project. A custom stain mix brought the planks to a creamy, beachy, light beige tone, perfectly suited to the coastal setting. Finished with extra-thick Bona Traffic HD in matte sheen, the result is a silky smooth, durable modern masterpiece of wide plank French oak for this Encinitas residence.

#75 French oak floor refinishing – Olivenhain Encinitas coastal ranch area This wide plank, long length French oak floor in an Olivenhain home near Rancho Santa Fe Road, El Camino Real, and Lucadia received a full transformation. We sanded away the texture and restored the floor to absolute flatness, using our Bona Portable Dust Containment Unit to ensure 100% dust-free sanding. A custom beige-toned stain gave the oak a modern, beachy look, and multiple coats of Bona Traffic HD matte finish created a smooth, durable surface built for coastal living near Encinitas Boulevard and the Pacific.

#78 Vintage Beech Wood Restoration – South Park, San Diego. This hundred-year-old beech hardwood floor in South Park had been worn down by decades of use and covered in a dark brown stain and polyurethane.
